This photograph,titled "Powerful Cloud",was
taken by Takashi for National Geographic and shows Japan's Mount Fuji capped with a rather spectacular lenticular cloud.These clouds are caused by the flow of stable,moist air over a physical obstacle,like a mountain peak. The air arises and then condenses and then sinks and evaporates,forming a capped,lentil-shaped cloud. This same process occurs in multiple layers above
the obstacle,causing a number of lenticular clouds to form at varying heights.
